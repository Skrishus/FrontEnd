The Fellowship of the Ring

From Tolkien Gateway

Overview

The Fellowship of the Ring is the first part of J.R.R. Tolkien's epic fantasy trilogy, The Lord of the Rings. The story follows the journey of Frodo Baggins as he sets out to destroy the One Ring and save Middle-earth from the Dark Lord Sauron.

Plot

Frodo Baggins, a young hobbit, inherits the One Ring from his uncle Bilbo. Realizing the danger it poses, Gandalf the Grey advises Frodo to leave the Shire and take the Ring to Rivendell. There, a council decides that the Ring must be destroyed by casting it into the fires of Mount Doom in Mordor. Frodo, along with eight companions, forms the Fellowship of the Ring and embarks on a perilous journey to save Middle-earth.

Main Characters

  • Frodo Baggins: The Ring-bearer, tasked with the mission to destroy the One Ring.
  • Gandalf the Grey: A wise wizard who guides and aids the Fellowship.
  • Aragorn: The rightful heir to the throne of Gondor and a skilled warrior.
  • Legolas: An Elven archer with unmatched skill.
  • Gimli: A stout and brave Dwarf warrior.
  • Samwise Gamgee: Frodo's loyal friend and gardener.
  • Meriadoc Brandybuck (Merry): A hobbit and close friend of Frodo.
  • Peregrin Took (Pippin): Another hobbit and companion of Frodo.
  • Boromir: A valiant warrior from Gondor.

Key Themes

  • The struggle between good and evil
  • The power of friendship and loyalty
  • The burden of responsibility and sacrifice
  • The corrupting influence of power
