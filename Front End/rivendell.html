Rivendell

From Tolkien Gateway

Introduction

Rivendell, also known as Imladris, is a fictional valley in J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-earth. It is an important location in his works, serving as a haven and refuge for the Elves. It was established by Elrond in the Second Age, and it played a crucial role in the events of both "The Hobbit" and "The Lord of the Rings".

Location and Description

Rivendell is located in the foothills of the Misty Mountains on the eastern side, near the sources of the Bruinen (Loudwater) and the river Hoarwell. It is described as a hidden valley that is protected and enchanted, offering a place of rest, healing, and learning.

Role in the Lore

Rivendell serves as a sanctuary for many characters in Tolkien's stories. It is where Bilbo Baggins ends his journey in "The Hobbit", and it is the place where the Fellowship of the Ring is formed in "The Lord of the Rings". Rivendell is known for its wisdom, council, and the preservation of history and lore.

Elrond, the master of Rivendell, is one of the most significant characters in Tolkien's mythology. His home is a place of rest and refuge for the weary, offering healing and comfort.

Interesting Facts

  • Rivendell was founded in the Second Age, around 1697.
  • It is also known as "The Last Homely House East of the Sea".
  • Rivendell remained a stronghold for the Free Peoples of Middle-earth against the forces of Sauron.

Inspiration

  • J.R.R. Tolkien was inspired by various myths and locations when creating Rivendell, including his own experiences in the Swiss Alps.
